Grading

Grade Components

15% Regular i>Clicker quizzes (given at lectures, lowest three dropped)
10% Participation in section activities (e.g., MultipliCITY modification, tool tutorials)
17%* Tabletop/resource game team document, draft project, playtest report, final project, and analysis
17%* Twine/link game team document, draft project, playtest report, final project, and analysis
17%* GameMaker/graphical game team document, draft project, playtest report, final project, and analysis
19%* Final project (game or essay) “beta” and final versions
5% Final exam and participation in final presentations

*Even if student has a numerical passing grade, course cannot be passed without receiving passing grades for all the assignments marked with asterisks. Exceptional final projects will receive extra credit.

Late Assignments

All assignment grades are reduced by 10% for each day late, to a maximum of 50%. Barring exceptional circumstances (which must be explained to your TA's satisfaction) no assignments more than one week late will be accepted. If you miss the lab at which your assignment was due, and there is an assignment component that was not uploaded into eCommons, it is your responsibility to deliver it to your TA's office. Analysis assignments allow resubmission. You may also be invited by your TA to re-do an assignment, in which case a specific due date will be provided. You cannot re-do an assignment that does not allow resubmission unless invited.

Quizzes

There will be no announcements of quizzes. Missing a quiz will result in no credit. Quizzes may cover any aspect of the course material, including readings, faculty lectures, guest lectures, material and activities covered in section, Twine, GameMaker, and so on.

Final Exam

Final examination details will be provided later in the quarter.

Final Presentations

A group of class "finalist" games will be chosen by the instructor, TAs, and reader/tutors, based on game quality and innovation. Finalists will receive extra credit. (Exceptional essays will also receive extra credit.) All finalist games will be presented to outside judges during course final exam period, with the best chosen to go on to the Sammy Awards. Attendance at final presentations is required.
